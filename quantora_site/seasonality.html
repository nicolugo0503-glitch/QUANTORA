Seasonality Explorer

Do some months treat a stock better than others? Enter a ticker and Quantora lays out its monthly returns year by year, then averages each calendar month and shows how often it finished green - the raw material behind "sell in May" and every other seasonal tell.

Each cell is that month's total return; the bottom rows show the average for that calendar month across all years and the hit rate (share of years it was positive). Patterns like a strong November-December or a weak September show up as columns of green or red. Treat them as curiosities, not certainties: with only a handful of years, apparent seasonality is often noise, it can vanish once discovered, and it says nothing about any single future month. It's a lens on a stock's history, not a calendar you can trade blindly.
Monthly returns from month-end closing prices (Financial Modeling Prep; may be delayed, cached in your browser for the day). Small samples - interpret with heavy caution. Educational analysis, not investment advice.
