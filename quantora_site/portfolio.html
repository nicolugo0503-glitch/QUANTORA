How it works
A portfolio's risk is not the average of its parts. Because positions move together, total volatility comes from the full covariance matrix, not just individual volatilities. Quantora computes portfolio variance as the weighted double-sum over every pair, then derives the metrics professionals watch:
σp² = Σi Σj wi wj σi σj ρij (portfolio variance) σp = √σp² (portfolio volatility) Sharpe = (Σ wiμi − rf) / σp (excess return per unit risk) Diversification ratio = (Σ wiσi) / σp (≥ 1; higher = more benefit) Risk contribution(i) = wi (Σj wj σi σj ρij) / σp²
The risk contribution is the key insight: a position can be a small slice of your capital yet drive a large slice of your risk. When the grey allocation bar and the purple risk bar diverge, that position is pulling more (or less) weight in risk than in dollars. The diversification ratio tells you how much the portfolio's correlation structure is reducing risk versus holding everything standalone.
