Risk Parity Allocator

Equal-weight isn't equal-risk - a volatile holding can dominate a portfolio's risk even at a small weight. Risk parity solves for the weights where every asset contributes the same share of total risk, the idea behind the famous "All Weather" approach. Enter tickers and Quantora computes it from real price history.

Each asset's risk contribution is its weight times how much it moves with the whole portfolio (its marginal contribution to volatility). Equal-weighting ignores this, so high-volatility or highly-correlated assets quietly carry most of the risk. Risk parity down-weights the volatile, correlated names and up-weights the calm, diversifying ones until every position pulls the same weight in risk terms - producing a more balanced, typically lower-volatility portfolio. It's not about maximizing return; it's about not being secretly concentrated. Practitioners often add leverage to lift the expected return back up, which carries its own risks.
