SEC Financials

A company's real numbers, straight from its SEC filings - not a data vendor's copy. Enter a US ticker and Quantora reads the audited XBRL data EDGAR extracts from every 10-K to chart revenue, profit, margins and the balance sheet over time.

Every public company files its financials in XBRL - a machine-readable format the SEC parses into a clean data feed. That's what powers this page, so the revenue and profit you see are the exact figures in the audited annual report, tagged by the filer, with no vendor in between. Charts show fiscal-year values from each 10-K. Net margin is net income over revenue - how many cents of each sales dollar become profit. Because companies choose slightly different accounting tags, coverage is best for large US filers; if a metric is missing, the company likely tags it under a different concept.
Data from SEC EDGAR XBRL company facts (public domain) via Quantora's server, no key. Fiscal-year figures from 10-K filings; some companies use non-standard tags, so a metric may occasionally be unavailable. For information and education, not investment advice.
