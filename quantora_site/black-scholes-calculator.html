How it works
The Black-Scholes-Merton model gives the fair value of a European option from six inputs: the underlying price, strike, time to expiry, volatility, the risk-free rate and dividend yield. The Greeks measure how that value changes - delta with the price, gamma with delta, vega with volatility, theta with time, and rho with rates.
